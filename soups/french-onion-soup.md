# French Onion Soup

*By Carol Pokodner Power*

A great classic translated directly from a French cookbook.

---

**Yields:** 4 servings | **Prep:** 30 mins | **Cook:** 1 hr | **Total:** 1 hr 30 mins | **Difficulty:** Advanced

---

## Ingredients

- ½ lb sweet (Vidalia) onions (about 3)
- 2 tbsp butter (¼ stick)
- 2 tsp flour
- 2 qts beef bouillon (can add water if needed)
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1 clove garlic, very finely chopped
- 1½ oz Gruyère cheese per bowl
- 8 slices French bread (or any crusty bread, about 1" thick)

## Directions

1. Peel and chop the onions very finely.

2. Melt the butter gently in a large, heavy-bottomed pot. Add the onions and let them cook until golden, stirring occasionally with a wooden spoon. Add the flour and continue cooking until the onions are browned and the flour is golden.

3. Add the beef broth and let cook for about ½–1 hour. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

4. Cut the bread on the diagonal into thin slices. Pan-fry the slices with ½ tbsp butter and the finely chopped garlic until the bread is toasted.

5. Alternate thin slices of bread and Gruyère cheese in the middle of your bowls. Fit as many slices as the bowl size allows.

6. Pour the hot soup on top of the bread and cheese — the bread will float. Cover with additional cheese on top.

7. Place in a hot oven (or under the broiler) until the cheese has a nice gratinéed crust and the bread is puffy.
