# Zucchini Stew

A hearty, lightly acidic stew with zucchini, lentils, and potatoes. The lentils thicken the broth as they set, and the apple-cider vinegar and soy sauce give the base a savory depth balanced by sage and thyme.

---

## Stovetop (2–4 servings)

### Ingredients

- 5–6 cups water
- 2–3 medium zucchini, sliced into rounds then quartered (2–3 cm pieces)
- 1 cup green or brown lentils
- 2–3 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed (1–1.5 cm pieces — keep them smaller than the zucchini so they don't overwhelm the palate)
- 1–2 cups frozen onion and bell pepper mix
- 2–3 tbsp apple-cider vinegar
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- Tony Chachere's seasoning (or salt + red pepper + black pepper), to taste
- 1 tsp dried sage
- 1 tsp dried thyme

### Directions

| Time | Step |
|------|------|
| T−50 | Pour water into a large pot and bring to a slow boil over medium-high heat. |
| T−45 | Add zucchini. |
| T−35 | Add flavors: apple-cider vinegar, soy sauce, and Tony's seasoning. Stir and taste. |
| T−25 | Add lentils and potatoes. |
| T−15 | Add frozen onion and bell pepper mix. |
| T−10 | Add sage and thyme to balance the acidity. |
| T±0  | Drop heat to a low simmer. |
| T+10 | Serve. Lentils will continue to set for at least 20 minutes after plating, thickening the stew as it sits. |

> **Tip:** Don't rush the simmer at the end — the lentils firm up and the flavors meld significantly in those last 10–20 minutes.

---

## Crockpot (6–8 servings)

### Ingredients

- 8–9 cups water
- 3–4 medium zucchini, sliced into rounds then quartered (2–3 cm pieces)
- 1½ cups green or brown lentils
- 3–4 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed (1–1.5 cm pieces)
- 2–3 cups frozen onion and bell pepper mix
- 3–4 tbsp apple-cider vinegar
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- Tony Chachere's seasoning (or salt + red pepper + black pepper), to taste
- 1½ tsp dried sage
- 1½ tsp dried thyme

### Directions

1. Add water, lentils, potatoes, apple-cider vinegar, soy sauce, and Tony's seasoning to the crockpot. Stir to combine.
2. Cook on **Low for 6–7 hours** or **High for 3–3.5 hours**, until potatoes are nearly tender and lentils have begun to soften.
3. Add zucchini and frozen onion/bell pepper mix. Stir in sage and thyme.
4. Cook for an additional **1 hour on Low** (or **30 minutes on High**), until zucchini is tender but not mushy.
5. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve — lentils will continue to thicken the stew as it sits.

> **Note:** Adding zucchini late keeps it from turning to mush. If you prefer it very soft, add it with the original ingredients.

---

## Notes

- Lentils do not need to be soaked. Brown or green lentils hold their shape better than red lentils; use red if you want a thicker, more puréed texture.
- Tony's seasoning is salt-forward — season lightly at first and adjust after flavors develop.
- A splash more apple-cider vinegar at the end can brighten a flat-tasting batch.
- Leftovers thicken considerably overnight. Add a splash of water when reheating.
